## User story

As a volunteer coach who has captured Maya through 11 weeks of practice — first as the kid
who couldn't read a closeout, then through the Week 4 "started using her left hand on the
drive" note, then through the Week 9 "finally cleared the help defender" moment — I want
one screen that lays Week 1 Maya next to current Maya side by side ("started: hesitated
on closeouts; now: closes out and recovers"), with the three turning-point observations
in between, so that when Maya's parent asks me at pickup "is she actually getting
better?" I have a real answer ready, and so the season's worth of captures I've put in
the app pay off as something I could not get anywhere else.

## Why now (four lenses)

### Product Owner
The product has shipped capture-time memory (0014 carryover, 0018 / 0020 arc threading,
0025 per-player capture memory at the moment of observing). It has shipped season-level
artifacts (0017 public season recap, 0036 wrap card on /home, 0034 cross-season parent
report continuity, 0059 cross-coach handoff). It has NOT shipped the single most
defensible per-player view in the product: the longitudinal "Week 1 → now" PER KID
trajectory, viewable in the coach's dashboard at any time, NOT only at season's end or
in a shipped artifact. Every other surface answers "what just happened" or "how was
this whole season"; nothing answers the question the coach actually gets asked at the
parking lot once a week per kid: "how is MY kid doing compared to where they started?"
The smallest meaningful unit of value is a `/team/[teamId]/player/[playerId]/trajectory`
page that reads the player's existing `observations` rows, picks the earliest grouped
`needs-work` cluster as the "started" anchor, picks the most recent `positive` cluster
on the same skill family as the "now" anchor, and selects 2-3 high-signal intermediate
observations as turning points. ONE AI call (`callAI` with a new `playerTrajectory`
prompt) renders the 4-6 sentences. Cached per `(player_id, observation-count-bucket)`
so re-opens are free. NO new data captured — the entire surface is built on
observations the coach has already entered.

### Stakeholder
This is the deepest expression of the "structured per-player memory the coach owns" moat
the product has. (1) The switching-cost moat — a coach who has 11 weeks of captures on
Maya and walks away from SportsIQ walks away from THIS screen, and there is nowhere else
to recreate it because no rival forms-app has the structured per-observation history. A
coach with two seasons of trajectory cards is, in a real sense, locked in to the value
they have built. (2) The tier-value moat — this is the screen the Coach tier ($9.99/mo)
needs to JUSTIFY itself for the coach who already loves the free capture: the free tier
gets 1 trajectory card preview per kid (read-once, screenshot-able for the parent at
pickup), the Coach+ tier gets the full set for every kid year-round. This is structurally
better tier-defense than "more AI calls per month" because it grows in value the longer
the coach uses the product. (3) The artifact-graph moat — the trajectory page is the
PARENT of three downstream artifacts the product already ships: parent report (0016 /
0034 continuity), spotlight (0009 / 0013), season recap (0017). Today those artifacts
read raw observations and re-derive the arc each time; once the trajectory exists, every
downstream artifact reads the SAME canonical trajectory as its starting point, so all
downstream artifacts about the same kid stay coherent.

### User (the coach, Saturday morning, 8:42am, on her phone in the kitchen)
She opens the SportsIQ app. From the roster she taps Maya. The player page already shows
the existing per-player capture memory (0025). New section above the recent-observations
list: "Maya — 11 weeks." Two columns, side by side: "Where she started" / "Where she is
now." Two sentences each, plus a single sparkline-style timeline along the bottom showing
the 3 turning-point observations as orange dots with one-word labels. Two more taps and
she has a 1280×720 portrait card ("Maya's growth — Hawks Spring 2026") generated by
`next/og` she can save to camera roll and show Maya's mom at pickup. If the player has
fewer than 4 observations the section reads "Maya's first observations are still being
written — come back after a few more practices" and the card is suppressed — silence
beats a half-evidence trajectory. If the coach is on the free tier and has already
viewed one trajectory in the last 30 days, the second tap surfaces the existing
`<UpgradeGate feature="feature_player_trajectory">` with benefit copy NAMED for this
coach's actual situation (Maya's first name, the observation count). One tap to upgrade,
and the trajectory expands inline; no page bounce, no re-derivation, no AI re-spend.

### Growth
The "show me" moment is the SCREENSHOT — a coach showing another coach the side-by-side
of Maya's started-vs-now at the bench during the game. That is the single most viral
artifact the product can ship because it requires having USED the product for 11 weeks;
no rival can fake the demo. Compounds three ways. (1) Each trajectory card the coach
shares with a parent at pickup is a soft acquisition vector (the card carries a
"made with SportsIQ" footer per the team-card precedent). (2) The trajectory is the
artifact most likely to lock a coach into a SECOND season — the moment the coach realizes
"if I keep capturing for another 11 weeks I get this AGAIN for next year's roster" is
the moment they upgrade their habit. (3) The downstream-artifact coherence means parent
reports and season recaps both become more polished by default, which raises the
conversion of every other already-shipped viral surface. Distinct from every shipped
surface: 0025 shows the LAST observation; 0018 / 0020 show the team's CURRENT ARC; 0017
shows the SEASON; THIS shows the KID's trajectory across the season, in one frame, in
one tap.

## Out of scope

- A team-level trajectory roll-up ("the whole team's started-vs-now"). v1 is per
  player; a team-level view is a follow-up ticket that reads the per-player cache.
- Cross-season trajectory (Week 1 of Season 1 → Week 11 of Season 2). v1 is
  within-season only; cross-season is what 0034 already covers as a parent report
  continuity surface and what 0059 covers on coach handoff. A combined cross-season
  trajectory is a follow-up.
- Live AI re-generation on every page open. v1 caches per
  `(player_id, observation_count_bucket)`; re-opens within the bucket return the
  cached row. The bucket advances every 3 observations.
- A "regenerate" button. v1 has no manual invalidation; the AI quota economics
  cannot afford it. The bucket-based cache is the SOLE refresh mechanism.
- Parent-facing trajectory (sending the trajectory to the parent automatically).
  v1 is coach-facing only; the OG card is a manual screenshot path. A parent-facing
  trajectory is the inverse of the SportsIQ trust model — the parent already has
  the parent report; this surface is the coach's tool for the parking-lot
  conversation.
- A trajectory for a player on a team the coach is an ASSISTANT on (not head).
  v1 is head-coach-only (matches the head-coach scoping on the existing per-player
  capture-memory and observation surfaces).
- A "share trajectory with the parent" public token. v1 creates no public
  surface; the coach screenshots the OG card and texts the parent manually. A
  public token would require its own privacy-page approval and a `parent_shares`-
  style row.
